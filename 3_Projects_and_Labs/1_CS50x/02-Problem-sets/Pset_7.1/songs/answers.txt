Answer to question 2:
This method might not be representative because a simple average treats a song played once the exact same as a song played a hundred times, easily destroying the fairness of the data. It also creates a confusing cluster of 6 traits without a clear priority order, ignoring factors like genre (rock vs. pop) or track length. 

A better approach would be to calculate a weighted average by multiplying each metric by the song's "play count" before averaging. This ensures the final aura heavily leans toward the user's actual favorite, most-listened tracks rather than casual skips.

And for an extreme calculation, we could even take the total song time for each specific category and then figure out the average and do another average or create a complex unique algorithm formula to calculate everything instead of just averaging all the time.
